####  Translations to English ####
#
#
#
#
# Goba' Keh - Be safe, good luck, similar meaning
#
# Egg - same as english, funny enough. 
# Igna Suh, Igna Suh. - colloquial phrase equivalent to "It is what it is."
# Yu I' - True God / The God / Monotheistic declaration
# Yu - A god / Deity
# Challa ki' - traditional semiformal greeting		
#Elj - Lord / Nobleman
#Moz'Ra - Sentinel / Armed Gatekeeper / Guardsman
#Gehru - King/Emperor
#Fla' - Strong / Physically Powerful
#Igna - Metal
#Ka Ni (Kah Nee) - From the / of the / taking the form of / like - such a common phrase it no longer has apostrophes to denote its pronounciation
#Terri' (Teh-Ree) - Wavelike / Flowing
#
#Chrali'Hajha - Painted Threshold
#Kalla' Eq' - Gatekeeper, keyholder, guardsman, doorwatch

####### PRONOUNCIATION #######
#
#An ' after a vowel means it is pronounced with a little more breath and more separate from what follows. Eh'Jan Ittla would be pronounced 'eh-jahn it-luh' (or itt-la for regional dialects.) Elji'a' would be 'ell-jee-ah'.
#Iltan has no hard "I" (eye) or "O" (owe) sound. They can pronounce it, they just don't.
